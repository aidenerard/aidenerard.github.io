About Me

Who I am, where I'm going, and how I plan to get there

Biography

I'm Aiden Erard, a first-year Computer Engineering student at Georgia Tech. I chose Computer Engineering because I didn't want to choose between hardware and software. I wanted to understand both.

Most of my time outside class goes into personal projects. I've built SOAR, an autonomous drone concept focused on public safety; GridGuard, a power monitoring system; and Tempr, an AI-powered music app that uses Gemini AI and the Spotify API to generate playlists from your mood, photos, or calendar. The project I've invested the most in is FluxSpace, an AI-powered data analysis platform for subsurface infrastructure inspection. The core idea is using technologies like GPR, ultrasound, and thermal imaging to collect data from structures like bridges, then running that data through AI and ML models to surface insights that would otherwise require a lot of manual interpretation. The goal is to make inspections faster, more accurate, and less dependent on human guesswork. It's a project I hope to continue long into the future and eventually start a company with.

On the academic side, I took CS 1301 last semester and I'm currently in CS 1331, building up my foundation in OOP through Java. The robotics labs in 1301 were extremely helpful in learning how to use Python in robotics settings. I programmed iRobot Create 3 robots to solve mazes and play pong.

I'm still figuring out where I want to focus, whether that's robotics, embedded systems, autonomous vehicles, or something I haven't gotten to yet. I want to keep building things that are useful and keep pushing myself technically.

Outside of that, I follow developments in AI and robotics and pick up new tools when something looks interesting. Traveling is something I genuinely love, whether it's somewhere new or somewhere I've been before. I'm planning to study abroad in Europe this summer and it's one of those things I know I will always do.

Career Goals

I'm a first-year student, so my goals are still taking shape. But after thinking a lot about what drew me to engineering and what excites me most about the field, here's where I think I'm headed and what I need to do to get there.

Long-Term Vision

Long term, I want to work on autonomous systems at a company doing meaningful technical work, whether that's in robotics, aerospace, vehicles, or some adjacent space. I'm also open to eventually starting something of my own if I find a problem worth solving. Graduate school is on the table too, especially if I find a research area I'm excited enough about to go deep into.

Timeline and Steps

Now through Freshman Year (2025-2026)

Build the technical foundation

Finish core CS coursework (1301, 1331), keep building personal projects, and start getting familiar with the ECE curriculum I'll hit in sophomore year. Focus on getting comfortable with embedded systems concepts and strengthen my C++ and Python skills.

Sophomore Year (2026-2027)

Get involved on campus and start applying

Join a project team (Robojackets or a similar GT engineering team) and look into undergraduate research through a lab or the ORS program. Start applying for summer internships in robotics, embedded systems, or software engineering. The goal is to get my first real engineering job experience.

Summer 2027

First technical internship

Target an internship at a company working on robotics, autonomous systems, or hardware-software integration. Even if it's more software focused, I want to be in an environment where I can learn from engineers doing work I respect.

Junior and Senior Year (2027-2029)

Go deeper technically and decide on next steps

Take advanced ECE coursework in controls, machine learning, and embedded systems. Continue research if it's going somewhere meaningful. Do another internship with more responsibility. By the end of senior year, have a clear answer on whether I'm going directly into industry or pursuing a master's or PhD.

Post-Graduation (2029+)

Full-time engineering and beyond

Work as a full-time engineer on autonomous systems or embedded hardware, at a company I believe in. Revisit the idea of grad school in a few years if there's a research direction I want to pursue. Longer term, stay open to building something of my own if the right opportunity comes along.

What I'm Focused on Right Now

  • Complete CS 1301 and CS 1331 with a strong understanding of the material, not just a grade
  • Keep building personal projects that push me into unfamiliar territory
  • Polish my resume and put together a strong portfolio before sophomore recruiting season starts
  • Start exploring Georgia Tech's engineering organizations and research programs to figure out where I want to get involved
  • Keep learning, whether that's a new language, framework, or hardware platform, something concrete every semester
